A Beginner's Guide To Rust

or, "wrangling with the borrow checker"

Welcome, {{ participant_name }}. This course started because the official Rust book is excellent and also a lot, all at once. I wanted something you could open in a browser, run, break, and keep moving — exercises that look like real code rather than toy problems, and a path through the language that doesn't dump every concept on you at once.

Nineteen short chapters. A handful of small projects along the way (a password validator, a .env parser, a tiny CSV parser). Tests that tell you when you're done. No setup required if you don't want any.

Take it at your own pace. Most chapters are five to ten minutes. A couple step up sharply on purpose — chapter 12 (iterators) and chapter 18 (a state machine for CSV) — and if you stall there, that's normal. Each has a Stuck? Show a hint disclosure tucked under the prose.
